Mental exhaustion can lead to a decrease in cognitive performance, making you more prone to mistakes and less able to process information effectively. This means that even if you force yourself to study, you might not retain the information as well as you would if your brain were rested.

Should I take a break?

Unfortunatly, time waits for no one. By the time you're done with your break, you'll feel more left behind than before. Long breaks can eat into your study time, reducing the overall time available for learning and potentially leading to cramming sessions later on. Extended breaks can disrupt the flow of study, making it harder to regain concentration and momentum once you return to your work.
